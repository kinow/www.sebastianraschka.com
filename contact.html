Sebastian is a PhD student at Michigan State University and is currently developing a novel drug screening software in the Protein Structural Analysis lab.

I was born and raised in Germany, where I did my Bachelor's degree in Biology (the more specific field for my thesis was developmental genetics) at Heinrich-Heine University, Duesseldorf.
In 2011, I joined an exchange program with Michigan State University (East Lansing, Michigan), and after I received my Bachelor's degree in Germany, I decided to come back to MSU to join a PhD program in Biochemistry, which recently became a Dual-Major degree: Computational Biology.
I always loved working with and tinkering around with computers, and thus I joined a computational protein structure analysis lab for my current PhD thesis, where I am developing software for protein-ligand docking and high-throughput drug screening (i.e., small chemical compounds for targetting protein receptors).

In my free time I started blogging about programming and bioninformatics, I like to share and talk about code and other useful things, and I have to say that I really enjoy both my work and my hobby.
